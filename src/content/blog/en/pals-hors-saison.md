Pals off-season is a different village. The medieval streets recover their calm, the beach opens wide without a soul in sight, the rice fields turn brown then tender green through the months. Between October and May, the Costa Brava reveals itself without the July-August crowds, at softer rates, with a more attentive welcome. Here is how to make the most of a stay at [Aparthotel Arenal](/en/) in low season, season by season, with concrete activities and practical landmarks.

## Why choose Pals off-season

Three main reasons. The climate first: the Costa Brava enjoys a microclimate that stays mild from September to November, with daytime temperatures between 18 and 24 degrees. Then the rates: a studio at [Aparthotel Arenal](/en/studios) costs two to three times less than in high season, with stays possible from one night. Authenticity finally: off-season, restaurants serve local clientele again, artisans have time to chat, hiking trails run empty.

For remote workers, this opens the door to extended workations. For couples, gastronomic escapes without booking three weeks ahead. For cycling enthusiasts, long rides without traffic.

## Autumn in Pals: September to November

September keeps the assets of summer plus cooler air. Sea water stays at 22-23 degrees, perfect for swimming until mid-October. Pals beach empties gradually after the second week of September, chiringuitos stay open with a calmer service.

October is harvest month in [Empordà vineyards](/en/blog/gastronomie-emporda). Several estates organise open-door days with participative harvests and tastings. The rice fields, harvested in September, give way to stubble that draws migratory birds. Coastal paths between Begur and Pals offer spectacular light.

November announces the mushroom season. Local markets fill with rovellons, restaurants propose tardor (autumn) menus around wild boar, duck and Pals rice. Temperatures stay pleasant during the day (14-18 degrees), evenings ask for a light sweater.

## Winter in Pals: December to February

Winter does not match the usual Costa Brava clichés. The thermometer rarely drops below 5 degrees at night and often reaches 12-15 degrees during the day. Sunny days are frequent, with a grazing light quality that photographers and painters especially appreciate.

Pals beach in winter has an austere, photogenic charm. Solitary walkers, line fishermen, a few runners at sunrise. The contrast with the summer beach is striking. Sunsets over the sea take on intense orange tones from December to February.

Nadal festivities liven up the villages. The Pals Christmas market, more intimate than Barcelona's, rewards the detour with local artisans and food products (La Bisbal turrón, Empordà wines, Arbeca olive oil). On 5 January, the Three Kings parade replaces Santa and marks a true moment of tradition.

January and February are the quietest months. This is the ideal period for a disconnection stay, with [studios](/en/studios) available at low rates (65 euros per night for a double studio at [Aparthotel Arenal](/en/), from 1 January to 28 February 2027). The region's starred restaurants offer tasting menus without rush and take the time to explain their cards.

## Spring in Pals: March to May

Spring is probably the best period to visit the Empordà. Between mid-March and mid-June, the region dresses up with wild flowers, almond and cherry trees offer spectacular blooms, temperatures range from 18 to 24 degrees.

The rice fields fill with water at the end of March, creating a mirror landscape unique in southern Europe. Birds return: purple herons, bee-eaters, passing flamingos. Professional photographers come every year to capture this light.

April and May are months for hiking and [cycling](/en/blog/vies-verdes-velo-pals). The Girona Vies Verdes can be ridden without excessive heat, with flower-rich landscapes along the way. The Camí de Ronda coastal paths between L'Escala and Palamós reveal empty coves and pure Mediterranean views.

Setmana Santa (Holy Week), mobile between late March and mid-April, brings special animation with processions in Verges, Easter markets and traditional menus around lamb and colomba.

## Key activities by season

The choice of activities changes radically from one season to another. Here is what really works well off-season:

- Hiking: the Cap de Creus trails, the Montgrí massif and the Camí de Ronda are perfect from October to May, with ideal temperatures for walking several hours
- Cycling: the [Vies Verdes](/en/blog/vies-verdes-velo-pals) work year-round, with minimal traffic on weekdays from November to March
- Wine tourism: Empordà wineries open by appointment with personalised tours and in-depth tastings away from the summer crowd
- Gastronomy: starred restaurants (El Celler de Can Roca, Castell Peralada, Els Tinars) take reservations more easily, seasonal menus use local produce
- Photography: autumn and spring light on rice fields, medieval villages and wild coast is exceptional
- Bird watching: the Aiguamolls de l'Empordà natural park hosts flamingos, storks, egrets and migratory raptors

## Seasonal gastronomy

Each season has its flagship products in the Empordà. Autumn brings mushrooms (rovellons, ceps, horns of plenty), game (wild boar, partridge, hare) and quinces. Winter celebrates calçots (grilled sweet onions) from February to April, Pals IGP rice in generous risottos, whelks and sea urchins on the coast.

Spring introduces wild asparagus, the first Maresme strawberries, grilled sardines (May). [Starred restaurants](/en/blog/gastronomie-emporda) adjust their menus every six weeks, offering a renewed experience at each visit.

Calçotadas (traditional meals around grilled calçots) happen in all the masies (farms) of the region from late January to early April. A real cultural moment, with mandatory bib and unlimited romesco sauce.

## How to reach Pals off-season

Access remains simple year-round:

- By car: AP-7 motorway exit 6 or 9, around 1h30 from Barcelona, 4h from Lyon, 6h from Paris
- By train: high-speed line Paris-Barcelona-Figueres, then car rental or bus at Figueres
- By plane: Girona-Costa Brava airport (30 minutes from Pals) or Barcelona El Prat (1h30)

Off-season, secondary roads are much quieter, medieval village car parks more accessible, travel times between points of interest shorter.

## Frequent questions on Pals off-season

### Does the climate stay pleasant in December-January?

Yes, as long as you are not looking for a swim. Sunny days dominate, daytime temperatures range from 10 to 16 degrees, with occasional warm days up to 20 degrees in February. Nights can drop to 3-5 degrees, Aparthotel Arenal is heated.

### Are restaurants open off-season?

The majority of quality restaurants stay open year-round, sometimes with a January closure (dates vary). Starred restaurants of the region operate 11 months out of 12. Beach chiringuitos generally close from November to late March.

### Can you swim in the sea in April-May?

Sea water is cool (14-17 degrees) but short swims are possible for regulars. Most visitors prefer to enjoy the beach for walking, running or picnicking. Comfortable swimming truly starts in late May-early June.

### Are there cultural events off-season?

Yes, and they are often more authentic than summer events. Pals Christmas market in December, Kings parade in January, calçotadas from February to April, Setmana Santa in spring, literary and music festivals in La Bisbal and Peratallada.

### Are museums and sites open?

The Dalí Theatre-Museum in Figueres, the Empúries archaeological museums, Peralada castle, the Romanesque churches of Palau-Sator: all operate year-round with slightly reduced hours from November to March. Online booking recommended for Dalí.

### What is the ideal off-season stay length?

A 3-night weekend allows discovering Pals and a neighbouring village. A week offers full immersion (medieval villages, coast, inland, gastronomy). Two weeks or more suit a workation, a photo project or deep disconnection. The flexibility of 1-2 night stays in very low season also allows short escapes.

### Should you rent a car off-season?

Strongly recommended. Public transport runs between larger towns but becomes limited to villages and remote sites. A car opens the whole region: wild Cap de Creus coast, volcanic Garrotxa, eastern Pyrenees, French border.
