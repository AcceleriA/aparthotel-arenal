The Costa Brava certainly lives up to its name. Between Blanes and the French border, the coastline alternates between rugged cliffs, hidden coves, and long sandy beaches. From Pals, a dozen beaches are accessible in less than twenty minutes.

## 1. Platja de Pals

The closest to Arenal, just five minutes by car. Three kilometers of fine sand bordered by natural dunes. The tramontane wind sometimes blows here, making it a favorite spot for kitesurfers. The northern section, toward the Gola del Ter, is the most wild.

## 2. Sa Riera (Begur)

A small pine-framed cove, fifteen minutes from Pals. The water here is remarkably clear. Parking is available at the top of the descent, and a few restaurants line the beach. Perfect for a half-day outing.

## 3. Aiguablava (Begur)

One of the most photographed beaches on the Costa Brava. The blue of the water here is almost unreal. The cove is small and very crowded in July-August. Best visited early in the morning or during the low season.

## 4. Platja del Racó

An extension of Platja de Pals, toward the south. Quieter and less developed. Access via a dirt road from the Begur route.

## 5. Cala Estreta (Palamós)

Accessible only on foot via the coastal trail (approximately 20 minutes walk). No services, no umbrellas. Just rocks, sea, and silence.

## 6. Platja de Llafranc

A fishing village transformed into an elegant seaside resort. The beach is small but very well sheltered. The coastal path toward Sant Sebastià lighthouse offers spectacular views.

## 7. Tamariu

A family-friendly cove with shallow and transparent waters. White houses of the village descend to the water's edge. Fresh fish restaurants complete the picture.

## 8. Platja de l'Estartit

Large beach facing the Illes Medes. Departure point for boat or kayak excursions to the archipelago, a protected marine reserve. Ten minutes from Pals.

## 9. Cala Pedrosa (Cap Roig)

A small wild cove accessible via the coastal trail from Calella de Palafrugell. Rounded stones instead of sand, water of absolute transparency.

## 10. Platja de Castell (Palamós)

The last truly virgin beach on the Costa Brava. No development along the shoreline. A secluded parking area, pine grove, and an arc-shaped beach. Twenty minutes from Pals.

## Practical tips

Costa Brava beaches are free. Parking is often paid in summer (5 to 10 euros per day). The most isolated coves are worth the effort: bring good shoes and water. The best season for beaches runs from mid-May to mid-October, with pleasant water temperatures from June through September.
