Pals is best discovered on foot. The old village perches on its hillside overlooking the Baix Empordà plain, nestled between the Montgrí massif and the Mediterranean Sea. You enter through the Torre de les Hores, a remnant of the 12th-century walls, and leave wanting to return immediately.

## The Medieval Village

The historic center of Pals was painstakingly restored stone by stone beginning in the 1950s. The result is striking: narrow streets of dressed stone, Gothic arches, and houses with sun-golden facades. The plaça Major hosts a lively market on Tuesday mornings from June to September, where locals and visitors mingle among fresh produce and crafts.

Named after the renowned Catalan writer native to the region, the [Josep Pla viewpoint](https://www.joseppla.cat) offers breathtaking 360-degree views over the rice fields, the Illes Medes, and Montgrí. Sunset remains the best time to make the climb, when the landscape transforms into shades of gold and amber.

For more about the region's character and attractions, visit the official [Costa Brava tourism portal](https://www.costabrava.org), which provides comprehensive information about villages, beaches, and cultural sites throughout the area.

## The Rice Fields of Pals

The Empordà is one of Catalonia's few rice-growing areas, and it has been cultivated here for centuries. The rice fields of Pals stretch between the village and the sea, creating a unique landscape that changes color dramatically with the seasons. In spring, flooded fields reflect the sky like mirrors. In summer, the intense green of the rice contrasts beautifully with the Mediterranean blue.

Pals rice has a protected designation of origin and appears on the menus of all quality restaurants in the region, particularly in arròs a la cassola, the emblematic dish of the Empordà. You can explore more about regional agriculture and the Empordà's agricultural heritage on [Catalunya.com](https://www.catalunya.com), which details the area's unique products and food traditions.

## Beaches and Nature

[Platja de Pals extends more than 3 kilometers](/en/environnement/plages) of fine sand, bordered by protected dunes and pine groves. Less crowded than the beaches of the southern coast, it retains a natural character that gives it undeniable charm. The dunes shelter protected flora and several hiking trails line the coastline.

The area is rich with natural preservation efforts. Visit [Parcsnaturals.gencat.cat](https://parcsnaturals.gencat.cat/ca/illes-medes) to learn about the [Illes Medes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medes_Islands), a protected marine reserve just offshore, which you can explore by boat or kayak from nearby villages.

Just a short drive away, the Sa Riera cove offers a more intimate alternative, with its dramatic rocks and crystalline waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

## Gastronomy

The Empordà concentrates a remarkable density of quality restaurants and culinary excellence. Pals boasts several recognized establishments, from gourmet restaurants showcasing Michelin standards to charming village bistros. Local products dominate the menus: Palamós shrimp, Pals rice, Escala anchovies, and award-winning Empordà wines.

The Pals market and the larger Torroella de Montgrí market allow you to shop directly from local producers, bringing home the authentic flavors of the region.
